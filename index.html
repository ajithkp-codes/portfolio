Uber Reserve: Diagnosing Low Adoption

üè¢ Uber üìã PM Assignment üî¨ User Research, JTBD

The Challenge

Uber Reserve allows users to pre-book rides for scheduled needs. Despite being live, adoption among urban professionals in Tier 1 cities remained low.

My task: Understand why users aren't adopting and propose a product improvement.

First Principles Analysis

What must be true for someone to use Reserve?

  1. They have a future, time-bound need (flight, meeting)
  2. They believe Reserve is more reliable than alternatives
  3. The price premium feels justified
  4. The experience is clear and reassuring

The Diagnosis

Trust Gap: "Will the driver actually show up?" "What if they cancel last minute?"

Mental Model Mismatch: Users expect "hotel booking" level confirmation. Reserve behaves like "we'll try to find a car at that time."

JTBD Framework

When I have an early-morning flight, I want to be absolutely sure I have a reliable ride waiting, so I can avoid anxiety about missing my departure.

Solution: "Uber Reserve Confidence"

FeatureUser Benefit
Early Hard Confirmation (12-4 hrs)Converts "hope" into visible plan
Driver Visibility & Pre-messagingMimics comfort of known taxi driver
Clear Backup GuaranteeReduces anxiety for critical trips
Transparent Premium ExplanationConnects price to tangible value

Key Takeaway

The problem wasn't awareness or pricing ‚Äî it was trust architecture. The solution converts hope into a visible, enforceable promise.
